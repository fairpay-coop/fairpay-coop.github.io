The Fairpay Cooperative was founded by Fabrice Armisen and Joseph Elliott in February 2016.   If you share our values and interested in joining or collaborating our organization, please contact us.

Joseph Elliott

Joseph is software architect and social entrepreneur.  As a veteran of the venture capital driven San Francisco Bay Area tech scene he passionately believes that there is a better way to structure enterprises to best serve customers, workers, and society.  He has found great inspiration from the books Reinventing Organizations by Frederic Laloux and Sacred Economics by Charles Eisenstein.

He is saddened by the unnecessary level of transaction fees typically paid out by community serving organizations.  The desire to help the organizations that he cares about to navigate the convoluted world of payment processing is what inspired him to found the Fairpay Cooperative.



Fabrice Armisen

Fabrice has more than 20 years of experience in software engineering and consulting, working with the complete lexicon of software technologies. Renowned for his resourcefulness, Fabrice loves nothing more than being told that something cannot be implemented, and then going away for a couple of hours and doing it.

When he's not crafting code, Fabrice can be seen rock climbing, riding his bike in the sunny streets of Oakland or improving his cooking skills in the kitchen.
